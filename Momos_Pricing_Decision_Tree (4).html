Pricing Defense Decision Tree

Hold price. Defend fees. Close without unnecessary concessions.

Core Principle — Annual Upfront Is the Standard

Annual upfront isn't a preference — it's how we price. The discounts embedded in our model assume annual cash collection. Customers who want flexibility are buying a premium optionality product, not a cheaper one. Your job is to make annual upfront feel like the obvious, smart choice — never a concession they're making.

① Set the Frame Before Objections Arise
▾

The best objection is the one that never happens. Frame annual upfront as the default early — never introduce it as an ask at the end of the deal.

Script — Frame on First Call

"Just so you know how we work — all our contracts are annual upfront. That's actually how we're able to offer the pricing we do. We'll get you the numbers based on that structure."

Script — Reinforce During Proposal

"This quote reflects annual upfront billing, which is our standard. It lets us fully commit the resources your account needs from Day 1."

Anchoring Psychology

Present annual upfront as the baseline, not the premium. Customers negotiate from the anchor you set. If you introduce monthly as an option, you've already created a "cheaper" alternative in their mind.

② Decision Tree — When They Push Back
▾

Customer says: "We prefer to pay monthly / quarterly" — New customer or renewal?

✓ New Customer

Move to Step 3: Cost of Flexibility conversation.

✗ Renewal

Step 3 — New Customer: Cost of Flexibility

Is the pushback about cash flow / budget cycle — or about trust / risk?

Cash Flow

Bridge with the investment framing. Show how annual upfront simplifies their budget as a single line item and eliminates monthly tracking overhead. Explore whether their CFO needs capex vs. opex framing.

Trust / Risk

Shorten scope (fewer locations, 1-year term) rather than changing payment terms. Keep annual upfront intact.

Script — Cash Flow Objection

"I hear you — let me reframe this. At [ACV], you're consolidating what used to be 3-4 separate tools into one line item. Most of our operators find it easier to get finance approval when it's positioned as a single annual platform investment rather than a recurring monthly charge. It's also simpler to track internally. Would it help if I put together a quick one-pager your finance team can use for budget approval?"

Core Principle — Price Is a Signal of Value

Every time you discount without a trade, you signal that your original price was inflated. Best-in-class AEs don't negotiate price — they negotiate scope, timeline, and terms. Protect your price; adjust the deal structure.

🚫 What To NEVER Do
▾
✗Never offer a discount before they ask for one. You're training them to always expect more.
✗Never say "I can give you X% off" as an opener. You've negotiated against yourself.
✗Never give a discount without getting something in return. A trade makes the discount feel earned, not extracted.
✗Never promise a discount that requires approval before you have it. It destroys your credibility if it falls through.
✗Never let a discount conversation die in email. Call them — urgency dies in async.
Core Principle — Every Year Added Is a Win-Win

Multi-year deals give customers real, meaningful savings built into the pricing structure. Your job is to make the math undeniable and the risk feel manageable. Always start multi-year. One-year is a fallback, not the ask.

🧠 The Best-in-Class AE Mindset
▾
Assume the Close

Write proposals as if they're signing. Present timelines, kickoff dates, onboarding steps. Confidence is contagious.

Urgency Must Be Real

Never manufacture fake urgency. Real urgency: pricing windows, onboarding cohorts, quarter-end team bandwidth.

Silence Is a Weapon

After presenting the offer, stop talking. The first person who speaks gives ground. Get comfortable with 3–5 seconds of silence.

Give to Get

Every concession should cost them something. Even a small trade — reference, case study, faster signature — changes the dynamic.

Go High Early

Always start at full price with standard fees. Starting at a discount signals you don't believe in your own pricing.

Bounded Choices

"Do you want to go with the 1-year or 2-year?" Not "What do you think?" Bounded choices accelerate decisions.

💬 Closing Scripts
▾
The "What Would It Take" Close

"I feel like we're close — I want to understand if there's something specific that would make this a clear yes for you. What would it take to get this done this week?"

The "Summary of Value" Close

"Let me recap what you're getting: [module recap], [contract discount for multi-year], CSM support at [tier], implementation covered in the onboarding fee, and kickoff on [date]. For [ACV] annually, you're getting [core outcome]. That math works, right?"

The "Competitive" Close

"I know you're also talking to [competitor]. I think the decision comes down to [key differentiator]. What would help you make that comparison cleanly?"

The "Hard Date" Close

"I want to be straight with you — if we can get the contract signed by [date], I can hold this pricing and get you into the [month] onboarding cohort. After that, I can't guarantee either. Does that timeline work on your end?"

The "Champion" Close

"It sounds like this is held up on [procurement/legal]. What does your internal champion need from me to move it forward? I can write the internal approval email for you if that helps — I've done it before."

🔄 CSM-Specific: Renewals & Expansion
▾
Start Renewals 90 Days Early

Never be in a renewal conversation with less than 60 days runway. Urgency dies, alternatives get explored, and budget cycles get complicated. 90 days = leverage.

Script — Proactive Renewal Opener (90 Days Out)

"I wanted to get ahead of your renewal — we have about 90 days, which is the right time to talk about what's working, where we're seeing the most value, and what the next 12–24 months could look like. Can we block 30 minutes?"

Expand Before You Renew

A customer who just added modules has much higher commitment to renewal. A customer debating renewal is a much harder sell for expansion. Always expand first.

Script — Expansion Pitch (CSM)

"Based on what I've seen in your account, [LM / next module] would directly address [specific pain point we've discussed]. I can put together a quick add-on quote — it's actually cheaper per location to add it now than to price it separately at renewal. Want me to run the numbers?"

Platform Fee Upgrade at Renewal

Renewal is the natural moment to upgrade CSM tiers. If the customer has been on Standard and you've been doing more informal work, formalize it into a paid tier. "You've been getting Pro-level attention — let's make it official and make sure you're fully supported."
