Pricing Defense Decision Tree

Hold price. Defend fees. Close without unnecessary concessions.

Rules That Apply to Every Deal
⚠

Annual contracts only. We do not offer monthly or quarterly billing. Any non-annual payment structure requires special approval from Sai.

⚠

7% annual price increase is standard. Every renewal above Standard tier includes a 7% annual increase. A customer can negotiate this down to the 3–5% range (inflation-adjusted), but never to 0%. If they want to hold pricing, the answer is a multi-year contract — the price locks for the full term.

⚠

No free trials. Ever. For Mid-Market: 30-day money-back guarantee (they pay upfront, full refund if they cancel within 30 days). For Enterprise: see the onboarding path below.

🏢 Enterprise Onboarding Path
▾

Enterprise customers don't get free trials — they get structured onboarding paths. Always start with Option 1 and only move down if there's genuine pushback. Each step requires more from us and less from them, so treat downward movement as a concession you're giving.

Option 1 — Default
Implementation Fee + Billing Starts
Full commitment. They pay the implementation fee and subscription billing begins immediately. This is the standard.
→
Option 2 — Pushback
Implementation Fee + 30-Day Opt-Out
They pay the implementation fee and billing starts, but they can opt out within 30 days for a refund on subscription (not implementation).
→
Option 3 — Sai Approval
Implementation Fee + Unpaid Pilot
They pay the implementation fee but get an unpaid pilot period before subscription billing begins. Requires Sai approval. They still pay for implementation.
Script — Presenting Option 1

"Our standard onboarding is straightforward — you pay the implementation fee to get everything set up, and billing starts on the same timeline. This is how we get you to value fastest, and it's how 90%+ of our enterprise customers start."

Script — Falling Back to Option 2

"I understand the hesitation. Here's what I can do — we'll start billing as normal, but I'll build in a 30-day opt-out window. If within the first 30 days you decide this isn't the right fit, we'll refund the subscription. The implementation fee stays because the work is already done at that point. That way you're protected, but we can still get you live immediately."

Script — Escalating to Option 3

"Let me take this to our CEO — for accounts like yours, he has the ability to structure a pilot where we cover the subscription cost for a defined period while you evaluate. You'd still pay the implementation fee since that covers real engineering time, but the subscription risk is on us. I'll need 24 hours to get this approved. Fair?"

📈 7% Annual Price Increase — Rules & Defense
▾
7%
Standard Annual Increase
Default for all renewals. This reflects platform improvements, expanded features, and market inflation.
3–5%
Negotiable Floor
If the customer pushes hard, you can negotiate to an inflation-adjusted 3–5%. Below this requires Sai approval.
0%
Multi-Year Lock
The only way to avoid annual increases is a multi-year contract. Price locks for the full term. This is how you redirect the conversation.
Never Agree to 0% on a 1-Year Deal

There is no scenario where a 1-year renewal has a 0% increase. If a customer says "we won't accept any increase," the answer is always: "Then let's lock your pricing in with a multi-year contract." This is a feature, not a concession — they get price certainty, we get commitment.

Script — Defending the 7% Increase

"Our standard annual adjustment is 7%, which reflects the ongoing investment we're making in the platform — new AI capabilities, expanded integrations, and enhanced support. Most operators see this as well below the value of what's being added each year. That said, if you want to lock in your current rate with no increases, the best path is a multi-year contract. Want me to run both scenarios?"

Script — Negotiating Down to 3–5%

"I can work with you on the specific number — we have some flexibility in the 3–5% range, which is essentially pegged to inflation. What I can't do is zero. If zero is truly what you need, the multi-year path is the only way I can make that happen. Which direction makes more sense for your team?"

Core Principle — Annual Contracts Only

We do annual contracts. Period. Annual upfront isn't a preference — it's the product. The discounts embedded in our model assume annual cash collection and commitment. ① Set the Frame Before Objections Arise
▾

The best objection is the one that never happens. Frame annual as the default early — never introduce it as an ask at the end of the deal.

Script — Frame on First Call

Anchoring Psychology

Present annual as the baseline, not the premium. Customers negotiate from the anchor you set. Never introduce monthly or quarterly as options — if you do, you've already created a "cheaper" alternative in their mind.

How the 20% Is Calculated — Your Justification

The implementation fee covers real engineering hours: integration configuration, data migration, location setup, QA testing, and team training. For a typical enterprise deal, this represents 40-80+ hours of dedicated engineering and onboarding time. At blended rates of $150-200/hr for technical implementation resources, the 20% target is often at or below our actual cost. This isn't a profit center — it's cost recovery for making sure the platform works on day one.

Customer pushes back: "Why do I have to pay an implementation fee?"

✓ They Want to Understand It

Walk through the hours. Use the engineering cost justification above. Make it tangible: "This is X hours of integration engineering at blended rates."

✗ They Want to Remove It

Do NOT offer to remove it. Explain what happens without proper implementation. If under 50 locations, you can offer the 30-day money-back guarantee path. If they push below 10%, escalate to Sai.

Script — Implementation Fee Value

"The implementation fee covers 40-80 hours of dedicated engineering and onboarding work — integration configuration, data migration, location setup, testing, and training. We price it at 20% of Year 1 ACV, which is actually at or below our blended engineering cost for this work. The customers who skip or minimize this step are the ones who see slower time-to-value and lower adoption in their first 90 days. It's a one-time cost that protects the investment you're making in the platform."

Script — When They Keep Pushing

"I hear you — it feels like an extra cost on top. But think of it this way: you're paying [ACV] per year for this platform to work. The implementation fee is what makes sure it actually does. We've seen operators try to skip this step and then spend months underutilizing the platform — that's the real cost. If the total contract value is the issue, let's look at contract length or modules — but the implementation fee stays in."

Anchoring Strategy: PLPM vs. Implementation

Customers almost never negotiate both the per-location price and the implementation fee equally hard. Early in the negotiation, figure out which one they care about more. If they're fixated on PLPM, hold firm there and know you have a little room on implementation (above the 10% floor). If they're fixated on implementation, hold that firm and know you can adjust scope or term to move PLPM. The key is: never give ground on both.

Pricing Psychology — Steering to Pro
Make Lower Tiers Feel Insufficient

Standard is not a dedicated CSM — it's customer operations support (ticket-based, reactive). Plus is a shared CSM — they're splitting attention across multiple accounts. This makes Standard feel like the outlier, not the default, and pushes the conversation to Plus vs. Pro rather than Standard vs. everything else.

Risk-Free Pro Trial

If the customer is between Plus and Pro: "Let me do this — we'll start you on Pro for the first month. You pay for it, but if after 30 days you feel Plus is enough, we'll refund the difference and downgrade you. That way you see what dedicated weekly CSM support actually looks like before you decide. 🚫 What To NEVER Do
▾
✗Never offer a discount before they ask for one. You're training them to always expect more.
✗Never say "I can give you X% off" as an opener. You've negotiated against yourself.
✗Never give a discount without getting something in return. A trade makes the discount feel earned, not extracted.

🧠 The Best-in-Class AE Mindset
▾
Assume the Close

Write proposals as if they're signing. Present timelines, kickoff dates, onboarding steps. Confidence is contagious.

Urgency Must Be Real

Never manufacture fake urgency. Real urgency: onboarding cohorts, CSM availability, pricing windows, quarter-end bandwidth.

Silence Is a Weapon

After presenting the offer, stop talking. The first person who speaks gives ground. Get comfortable with 3–5 seconds of silence.

Give to Get

Every concession should cost them something. Even a small trade — reference, case study, faster signature — changes the dynamic.

Go High Early

Always start at full price with standard fees and 20% implementation. Starting at a discount signals you don't believe in your own pricing.

Bounded Choices

"Do you want to go with the 1-year or 2-year?" Not "What do you think?" Bounded choices accelerate decisions.

"It sounds like this is held up on [procurement/legal]. What does your internal champion need from me to move it forward? I can write the internal approval email for you if that helps — I've done it before."

The "Sai Alignment" Close

"Look, you and I are aligned on this — I want to make this work. What I need to do is bring this to our CEO within 24 hours for approval. He reviews these personally. If he signs off, are we moving forward? Give me that commitment and I'll go to bat for you."

🔄 CSM-Specific: Renewals & Expansion
▾
Start Renewals 90 Days Early

Never be in a renewal conversation with less than 60 days runway. Urgency dies, alternatives get explored, and budget cycles get complicated. 90 days = leverage. And remember: the 7% increase is coming — use it to drive multi-year conversations early.

Script — Proactive Renewal Opener (90 Days Out)

"I wanted to get ahead of your renewal — we have about 90 days, which is the right time to talk about what's working and what the next 12–24 months could look like. I should mention upfront that our standard renewal includes a 7% adjustment, but if we do a multi-year renewal, we can lock in your current rate. Can we block 30 minutes to walk through the options?"

Expand Before You Renew

A customer who just added modules has much higher commitment to renewal. A customer debating renewal is a much harder sell for expansion. Always expand first.

Script — Expansion Pitch (CSM)

"Based on what I've seen in your account, [LM / next module] would directly address [specific pain point we've discussed]. I can put together a quick add-on quote — it's actually cheaper per location to add it now than to price it separately at renewal. Want me to run the numbers?"

Platform Fee Upgrade at Renewal

Renewal is the natural moment to upgrade CSM tiers. If the customer has been on Standard and you've been doing more informal work, formalize it. "You've been getting shared CSM-level attention on a Standard plan — let's make it official with Plus or Pro so you're fully supported. Most operators your size are on Pro."
